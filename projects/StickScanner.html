Package Label Verification

Project information

  • Category: Computer Vision
  • Client: West Fraser
  • Project date: October 8, 2019 to October 28, 2019

A larger project for Iron Code and myself was the introduction of a stick scanning system to the list of available options to Saw Mill Stackers. Between the layers of wood coming from the sawmill sticks are placed to allow airflow in the kilns and increase stability of the package. These sticks in high speed lines are placed automatically by the stacking machines. However due to the stresses of heavy loads and the strain that repeated trips through kilns takes on the sticks they warp and are only suitable for use a certain number of times. This project aimed to take 3d scans of the sticks prior to going into the hoppers at the stacker to reject sticks that wouldn't making it through the system or would cause instability in the stacked product.

3d cameras from LMI (goCators) were selected to get a point cloud of data. from this point cloud the viability of the stick was determined by comparing the readings to user selectable criteria. A solution is provided from the camera system and sent back to the stacker PLC for further processing of the stick. This process of scan, compute, decision can happen as quick as 5 times a second.
