About me

Hello, I’m Kelvin, an experienced electrical and controls engineer with a strong background in motion control, industrial automation, and multidisciplinary systems integration.

I’ve spent over a decade designing, programming, and deploying control systems across sectors including marine, manufacturing, and heavy industrial environments. My core expertise includes PLC programming (Allen-Bradley, Siemens, ABB), HMI/SCADA development, and the implementation of safety-critical systems meeting standards like SIL, HAZOP, and LOPA.

I’m particularly strong in actuator and drive systems — electrical, hydraulic, and pneumatic — and have worked extensively with industrial communication protocols such as Ethernet/IP, Modbus, Profibus, Profinet, ControlNet, and RS485. I've also led or contributed to the development of detailed technical documentation, from control narratives and schematics to commissioning procedures and regulatory compliance reports.

While I don’t consider myself a web developer, I created this site as a minimal way to maintain a professional web presence and make it easier to share a consistent overview of my background. You’ll find a summary of my experience, selected project highlights, and contact details for further discussion.

I’m years old, currently based in Port Coquitlam British Columbia, and open to opportunities that require a practical, systems-oriented thinker who understands how real-world machines, people, and data interact.
